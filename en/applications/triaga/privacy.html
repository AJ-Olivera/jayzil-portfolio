Privacy Policy

Effective July 21, 2026. This policy covers the Triaga mobile application only.

Summary

Triaga is designed to work entirely on your device. It does not require an account, does not sync to a server, and does not share your information with third parties. This page explains what that means in detail, including the permissions Triaga requests and how Premium subscriptions are billed.

Information we collect

Triaga does not collect personal data on our servers. Medication names, dosages, schedules, notes, and reminder history — including an optional patient name used for PDF sharing — are stored locally on your device only, in the app's own local database. Jayzil Studios has no access to this information and cannot view, retrieve, or recover it.

Data security

This data is protected by your device's own security, such as your lock screen or full-device encryption if you have that enabled at the operating system level. Triaga does not currently add its own independent encryption layer on top of standard device storage.

Accounts and sign-in

Triaga does not require or offer account creation. There is no login, and no personal identifiers (such as name or email) are collected through the app.

Backups

Triaga does not sync data to Jayzil Studios or any third-party cloud service, and keeps no server-side backup of your information. Backup & Restore, in the app's Settings, lets you manually export your data to a file at a location you choose, and restore it later — for example when switching to a new device. That file is created and shared only through your own device's share sheet, and is never uploaded anywhere by Triaga itself. If your device's operating system includes its own backup feature (such as a system-level cloud backup), that backup is managed by your device manufacturer, not by Jayzil Studios.

Premium subscriptions and payments

Triaga offers an optional Premium subscription that unlocks additional features, such as PDF sharing of medication history, additional themes, and up to 50 active reminders. Premium is billed and managed entirely through the Google Play Store: Jayzil Studios does not collect, process, store, or have access to your banking, credit card, or other payment information. Subscriptions renew automatically at the end of each billing period unless cancelled at least 24 hours before renewal, and can be viewed, changed, or cancelled at any time from Google Play's own subscription settings. If a Premium subscription ends, reminders beyond the free plan's limit are paused — not deleted — for as long as the app remains installed, and resume automatically if Premium is reactivated.

Third parties and analytics

Triaga does not integrate third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services of any kind. The only third party involved in using Triaga is the Google Play Store, which processes Premium subscription payments and distributes the app on Android — see Google's own Privacy Policy for how it handles that information. No data is sold or shared with any other third party.

Permissions

Triaga requests a small number of device permissions, each used only for the purpose described here: notification permission, so the app can alert you at scheduled medication times; and permission to schedule exact-time alarms, which keeps reminder timing reliable on Android. Two further permissions are entirely optional, requested only if you choose to enable them from Critical Alerts in Settings — access to your device's Do Not Disturb settings, so a critical reminder can break through that mode (sound still depends on your phone's own ringer and volume, as you've set them), and an exemption from battery optimization, which further reduces the chance of a delayed notification. None of these permissions give Triaga access to any other data on your device.

Data deletion

Because all data is stored locally, uninstalling the app — or clearing its data/cache from your device's settings — permanently deletes all Triaga data. Jayzil Studios keeps no server-side copy, so data cannot be recovered after removing or replacing a device unless you created a Backup & Restore file beforehand.

Children's privacy

Triaga is not directed at children or teenagers, and its use assumes the legal capacity to accept these terms. If the app is used by a minor, this should only occur under the supervision and responsibility of a parent or legal guardian, who is solely responsible for any information entered and for the app's use on the minor's behalf.

Applicable law

Because Triaga does not collect, transmit, or process personal data on its own servers or third-party servers — all information stays exclusively on the user's device — the data-controller obligations of Brazil's General Data Protection Law (LGPD, Law No. 13,709/2018) do not apply to Jayzil Studios with respect to this data. This policy is governed by the laws of the Federative Republic of Brazil.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, an updated version will be posted on this page and in the app, with a revised effective date. Continuing to use Triaga after a change means you accept the updated policy.
